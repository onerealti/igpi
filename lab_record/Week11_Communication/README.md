# Week 11: Communication & Documentation

## Aim
To compile professional engineering documentation including a final technical report, a user assembly manual, a project poster outline, and a product pitch summary.

## Topics Covered
*   Technical Report Structure
*   Clear User Manual Design
*   Visual Poster Presentation Layout
*   Technical Pitch Structuring ("The Helio-Track S1")

---

## Activity

### 1. Technical Design Report Outline
We structured our final technical paper using the following outline:
1.  **Abstract:** Summary of the project, dual-axis solar tracking method, and the recorded efficiency gains (32% increase) over fixed panels.
2.  **Introduction:** Background on off-grid energy generation and limitations of fixed installations.
3.  **Design Methodology:** CAD modeling details, 3D printing parameter selections, circuit designs, and Arduino software hysteresis logic.
4.  **Testing and Results:** Comparative charts of light intensity vs. output voltages and power consumption measurements.
5.  **Sustainability and Life Cycle Impact:** Energy payback analysis comparing 3D printing plastic footprint against energy harvested.
6.  **Conclusion & Future Scope:** Final recommendations for scaling the mechanism to larger panels (e.g. 100W+ arrays).

### 2. User Assembly & Operating Manual
1.  **Assembly:** Align the Main Pillar with the Base Plate socket and secure the bottom joint with the provided M4 socket screws.
2.  **Wiring:** Plug the 4-pin LDR header connector into the marked "Sensor" port on the controller box.
3.  **Power Connection:** Connect a regulated 5V DC (minimum 2A) power supply to the USB-B port or power terminal on the base box.
4.  **Calibration:** If the panel does not face the light source, use a small screwdriver to turn the sensitivity potentiometers on the LDR sensor module until panning is smooth.
5.  **Maintenance:** Every 3 months, wipe the LDR lenses with a clean microfiber cloth to remove dust and check the servo screws to make sure they are tight.

### 3. Poster Presentation Layout
Our project poster outline contains:
*   **Problem Statement:** Large graphs showing energy lost by static solar arrays.
*   **System Architecture:** CAD assembly screenshots and the wiring schematic.
*   **Testing Logs:** Graph comparing hourly power output of a fixed 10W panel vs. our tracking 10W panel.
*   **Key Specs:** List of weights, servo torque safety margins, and total cost breakdown.

### 4. Technical Pitch: "The Helio-Track S1"
*   **The Problem:** Fixed solar panels lose up to 40% of potential daily solar energy because they only align with the sun at noon. For off-grid cabin owners and van-lifers, this means buying extra, expensive panels.
*   **The Solution:** The Helio-Track S1 is a 3D-printable, low-cost ($50) dual-axis tracking mount that automatically tracks the sun.
*   **The Value Proposition:**
    *   *Efficiency:* Boosts daily energy generation by over 30%.
    *   *Cost-Effective:* Uses common, easily sourced components that can be printed and repaired anywhere.
    *   *Smart Logic:* Automatically resets to face East at sunset and sleeps during night/low-light states to save power.
*   **Closing statement:** *"Why settle for 60% of your power when you can have 100%? The Helio-Track S1: Making every ray count."*
